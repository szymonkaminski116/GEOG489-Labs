The four maps above represent the overall outcomes of the US Presidential Election 2020. In the first map, it displays how the rural area of the United States is sparsely populated, yet the Electoral College grants them the same amount of power as the heavily populated urban areas that are sparsely scattered throughout the country. Former President Trump may have won more land, but President Biden won the popular vote. The second map displays an overall increase voter turnout since 2016. Both presidential candidate received a record amount of votes in U.S. history and it displays the competitiveness of both campaigns to win. Furthermore, it shows the success to helpe people pursue early/absentee voting as well as enfranchising those who previously didn't vote in 2016. The third map displays how much the margin of victory for a candidate shifted in a county since 2016. President Biden was able to win more counties in battleground states like Wisconsin, Pennsylvania, and Arizona that Clinton lost. The bigger the arrow means the bigger shift in margin of victory that the county had. The last map displays how most US counties are still purple. It respresents how the majority of people are in the middle with both sides not fully representing their views. The two-party system in the United States gives an illusion that counties are totally one-sided, even though a candidate would win the county by barely winning over 50% of the votes. Ultimately, the maps display the faults of the first past the post voting system that the United States has. It calls for action to change the status quo in order to ensure people's views are accurately represented in government and politics.
